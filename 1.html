Matrimony

Two in one flesh

 God created man and woman in His own image and likeness. Both possess the same human nature and are meant to complement each other. They were called to transmit life to future generations. New life should be the fruit of the mutual love of the spouses, being a reflection of the love of God the Creator. From the beginning of human history, marriage has been a sacred thing.

A great mystery
in reference to Christ
and the Church

 Already in the Old Testament, the covenant of God with His chosen people was compared to the matrimonial union. Marriage is also an image of the union between Christ and the Church. Christ loved the Church as His bride and died for her. This love is fruitful, for the Church has become the mother of all the redeemed.
 The mutual self-giving of Christ and the Church is the model of true matrimonial love, which consists in mutual sacrifice. The woman offers the sacrifice of her grace and the power of her love; the man sacrifices his freedom and all his efforts to provide a happy life for his chosen one. To this sacrifice must be added all the labors and cares of common life and the education of offspring. Therefore, it is the wish of the Church that the celebration of marriage be joined with the Holy Mass, during which the newlyweds unite their sacrifice with the sacrifice of Christ the Lord.

Matrimonial impediments

 In order to protect the spouses and their offspring from temporal and supernatural harm, Almighty God forbade marriage in certain circumstances. For the same reasons, the Church established further matrimonial impediments. Some of these make the contracting of a valid marriage impossible, while others require a dispensation, which the Church grants for grave reasons.
