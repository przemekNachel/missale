Małżeństwo

Two in one flesh

 God created man and woman in His image and likeness. Both share the same human nature and are meant to complement one another. They are called to pass on life to future generations. New life should be the fruit of the mutual love of spouses, reflecting the love of God the Creator. From the very beginning of human history, marriage has been considered something sacred.

A great mystery in relation to Christ and the Church

 Already in the Old Testament, the covenant between God and the chosen people was compared to a marital union. Marriage is also an image of the relationship between Christ and the Church. Christ loved the Church as a bride and died for her. This love is fruitful, for the Church has become the mother of all the redeemed. The mutual self-giving of Christ and the Church is a model of true marital love, which consists in mutual sacrifice. A woman offers her grace and the power of her love; a man sacrifices his freedom and all his efforts to provide a happy life for the one he has chosen. To this sacrifice must be added all the hardships and concerns of shared life and the raising of children. Therefore, it is the wish of the Church that the celebration of marriage be united with the Holy Mass, during which the newlyweds join their offering with the sacrifice of Christ the Lord.

Impediments to marriage

 In order to protect spouses and their offspring from temporal and spiritual harm, God has forbidden marriage in certain circumstances. For the same reasons, the Church has established additional marital impediments. Some of these make a valid marriage impossible, while others require a dispensation, which the Church grants for serious reasons.

Contracting marriage
