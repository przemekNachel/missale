Gradual and Alleluia

After the Epistle the choir sings the text of the inter-lectionary chant called the Gradual. This text is mostly taken from the Psalter. Alleluia - a Hebrew word meaning "praise the Lord" - is a cry of joy flowing from the heart of the Church because of God's benefits.

GRADUALPs 127:3

 Thy wife shall be as a fruitful vine on the sides of thy house. V. Thy children as olive plants round about thy table.
 Uxor tua sicut vitis abundans in lateribus domus tuae. V. Filii tui sicut novellae olivarum in circuitu mensae tuae.

ALLELUIAPs 19:3

 Alleluia, alleluia. V. May the Lord send you help from His sanctuary: and defend you out of Sion. Alleluia.
 Alleluia, alleluia. V. Mittat vobis Dominus auxilium de sancto: et de Sion tueatur vos. Alleluia.

Preparation for the Gospel

After the inter-lectionary chant, the priest, bowing low before the center of the altar, prays (Is 6):

 Cleanse my heart and my lips, O Almighty God, who didst cleanse the lips of the prophet Isaias with a burning coal; through Thy gracious mercy vouchsafe so to purify me that I may worthily proclaim Thy holy Gospel. Through Christ our Lord. Amen.
 Munda cor meum ac labia mea, omnípotens Deus, qui labia Isaíæ Prophétæ cálculo mundásti igníto: ita me tua grata miseratióne dignáre mundáre, ut sánctum Evangélium tuum digne váleam nuntiáre. Per Chrístum Dóminum nóstrum. Amen.
 Pray, Lord, a blessing. The Lord be in my heart and on my lips, that I may worthily and fittingly proclaim His Gospel. Amen.
 Iube, Dómine benedícere. Dóminus sit in corde meo et in lábiis meis: ut digne et competénter annúntiem Evangélium suum. Amen.

The Gospel

During a High Mass, the priest moves to the Gospel side and sings it, having previously signed the missal, his forehead, lips, and breast with the sign of the cross.
 

The faithful stand.
