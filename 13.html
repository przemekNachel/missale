The priest bows over the Host. 

HOC EST ENIM CORPUS MEUM.

FOR THIS IS MY BODY.

As the priest elevates the Sacred Host, the server rings the bell, and the faithful adore in silence. 

Consecration of the Wine

The priest takes the Chalice into his hands. 

Simili modo postquam cœnatum est, accipiens et hunc præclarum Calicem in sanctas ac venerabiles manus suas: item tibi gratias agens, bene dixit, deditque discipulis suis, dicens: Accipite, et bibite ex eo omnes.
In like manner, after He had supped, taking also this excellent Chalice into His holy and venerable hands: likewise giving thanks to Thee, He bles sed, and gave it to His disciples, saying: Take and drink ye all of this:

The priest bows over the Chalice. 

HIC EST ENIM CALIX SANGUINIS MEI, NOVI ET ÆTERNI TESTAMENTI: MYSTERIUM FIDEI: QUI PRO VOBIS ET PRO MULTIS EFFUNDETUR IN REMISSIONEM PECCATORUM.

 Hæc quotiescumque feceritis, in mei memoriam facietis.

FOR THIS IS THE CHALICE OF MY BLOOD, OF THE NEW AND ETERNAL TESTAMENT: THE MYSTERY OF FAITH: WHICH SHALL BE SHED FOR YOU AND FOR MANY UNTO THE REMISSION OF SINS.


 As often as ye shall do these things, ye shall do them in remembrance of Me.

As the priest elevates the Chalice, the server rings the bell; the faithful adore the Most Precious Blood in silence. The priest recites the prayer for the acceptance of the unbloody Sacrifice and the prayer of intercession.
  Then he uncovers the chalice and makes the sign of the cross five times with the Holy Host, then elevates the chalice along with the Host. At the same time he glorifies God, saying:

 Per ipsum , et cum ipso , et in ipso , est tibi Deo Patri omnipotenti, in unitate Spritus Sancti, omnis honor et gloria.
 S. Per omnia sæcula sæculorum.
 M. Amen.
 Through Him, and with Him, and in Him, is unto Thee, God the Father almighty, in the unity of the Holy Ghost, all honor and glory.
 K. World without end.
 W. Amen.

The Lord's Prayer

Now, after the offering of the Sacrifice, the banquet follows. In response to our gifts, petitions, and supplications, God will grant us the holy food in which all graces and benefits are contained, for this food is the Lord Jesus Himself under the species of bread and wine. This is a confirmation that God has heard us and wishes to help us as His children. As preparation for Holy Communion, let us say from the bottom of our hearts the "Our Father" and ask the Lord Jesus that this His Body, which we are about to consume, may become for us a medicine for soul and body.
