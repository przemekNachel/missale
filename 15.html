 Sit amabilis viro suo, ut Rachel, sapiens, ut Rebecca, longaeva et fidelis, ut Sara, nihil in ea ex actibus suis ille auctor praevaricationis usurpet, nexa fidei mandatisque permaneat, uni thoro iuncta, contactus illicitos fugiat, muniat infirmitatem suam robore disciplinae, sit verecundia gravis, pudore venerabilis, doctrinis caelestibus erudita, sit fecunda in sobole, sit probata et innocens, et ad Beatorum requiem atque ad caelestia regna perveniat, et videant ambo filios filiorum suorum, usque in tertiam et quartam generationemn, et ad optatam perveniant senectutem.




 Per eundem Dominum.
 R. Amen.
 May she be amiable to her husband as Rachel, wise as Rebecca, long-lived and faithful as Sara. Let not the author of deceit have any share in her actions; let her remain constant to the faith and the commandments. United to one husband, let her flee all unlawful addresses. Let her fortify her weakness by strong discipline; let her be grave in her modesty, venerable in her reserve, and learned in heavenly doctrine. Let her be fruitful in offspring; let her be approved and innocent, and let her attain to the rest of the Blessed and to the heavenly kingdom. May they both see their children's children unto the third and fourth generation, and reach a desired old age.
 Through the same Lord.
 R. Amen.

They then return to their places. The priest turns to the altar and continues the Holy Mass. 

Fraction of the Bread and prayers for peace

The rite of the Fraction of the Bread is also a symbol of Christ's violent death, while the commingling of the Host with the Blood points to His resurrection.
 The priest breaks the Host into three parts, saying:

 S. Per omnia sæcula sæculorum.
 M. Amen.
 K. World without end.
 W. Amen.

The priest drops a particle of the Holy Host into the Chalice, saying: 

 S. Pax Dómini sit semper vobís cum.
 M. Et cum spíritu tuo.
 S. Hæc commixtio et consecratio Corporis at Sanguinis Domini nostri Iesu Christi fiat accipientibus nobis in vitam æternam. Amen.
 K. May the peace of the Lord be always with you.
 W. And with thy spirit.
 S. May this commingling and consecration of the Body and Blood of our Lord Jesus Christ be to us who receive it effectual unto life everlasting. Amen.

The faithful kneel. The priest, bowing and striking his breast three times, says: 

Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccáta mundi, miserére nobis.

 Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccáta mundi, miserére nobis.
 Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccáta mundi, dona nobis pacem.
Lamb of God, who takest away the sins of the world, have mercy on us.
 Lamb of God, who takest away the sins of the world, have mercy on us.

 Lamb of God, who takest away the sins of the world, grant us peace.
