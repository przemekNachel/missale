Prayers after Communion

 S. Dóminus vobíscum.
 M. Et cum spíritu tuo.
 S. Orémus.
 K. The Lord be with you.
 W. And with thy spirit.
 K. Let us pray.

POSTCOMMUNION

 Quaesumus, omnipotens Deus: instituta providentiae tuae pio favore comitare; ut, quos legitima societate connectis, longaeval pace custodias. Per Dominumn.
 We beseech Thee, almighty God, graciously to accompany with Thy fatherly favor the union instituted by Thee, and to preserve in lasting peace those whom Thou hast joined in lawful matrimony. Through our Lord.

 The priest reads the Postcommunion prayer. Let us give thanks together with the priest to God, that He has deigned to nourish us with the Body of His Son, Jesus Christ, and let us ask that this Holy Communion may give us strength to fulfill our duties and lead us to eternal life through the same Christ our Lord, who with God the Father lives and reigns in the unity of the Holy Ghost.

 S. Per omnia sæcula sæculorum.
 M. Amen.
 K. World without end.
 W. Amen.

 After the prayers are finished, the priest returns to the center of the altar, kisses it, and turning to the faithful, says:

 S. Dóminus vobíscum.
 M. Et cum spíritu tuo.
 S. Ite, missa est.
 M. Deo grátias.
 K. The Lord be with you.
 W. And with thy spirit.
 K. Go, the Mass is ended.
 K. Thanks be to God.

After the Ite, missa est, the newlyweds again approach the altar, and the priest says over them the following prayer:

 Deus Abraham, Deus Isaac, et Deus Iacob sit vobiscum: et ipse adimpleat benedictionem suam in vobis: ut videatis filios filiorum vestrorum usque ad tertiam et quartam generationem, et postea vitam aeternam habeatis sine fine: adiuvante Domino nostro Iesu Christo, qui cum Patre et Spiritu Sancto vivit et regnat Deus, per omnia saecula saeculorum. Amen.
 May the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob be with you; and may He fulfill His blessing in you, that you may see your children's children unto the third and fourth generation, and thereafter possess life everlasting without end: through the help of our Lord Jesus Christ, who with the Father and the Holy Ghost lives and reigns, God, world without end. Amen.

The priest sprinkles the newlyweds with holy water, after which he concludes the Holy Mass.

Last Prayer

The faithful kneel. The priest, bowing over the altar, says: 

Placeat tibi, sancta Trinitas, obsequium servitutis meæ: et præsta, ut sacrificium quod oculis tuæ maiestatis indignus obtuli, tibi sit acceptabile, mihique, et omnibus proquibus illud obtuli,
O Holy Trinity, may the homage of my service be pleasing to Thee; and grant that the sacrifice which I, though unworthy, have offered in the sight of Thy majesty, may be acceptable to Thee, and through Thy mercy avail me and all those for whom I have offered it,
