In extraordinary cases, namely in danger of death or when it is foreseen that for a period of one month it will not be possible to have access to an authorized priest, the newlyweds, being free from impediments, may enter into a sacramental marriage before two witnesses. They may use the words of the matrimonial oath contained in the rite of marriage.

Graces of the Sacrament of Matrimony

The spouses receive an increase of sanctifying grace and a constant right to the actual graces necessary throughout their married life to preserve mutual love and to raise their offspring. The faithful fulfillment of these duties is for the spouses a means of growth in the love of God.

Rites of the Sacrament of Matrimony

Hymn Veni Creator

Come, Holy Ghost, Creator blest, Vouchsafe within our souls to rest; Come with Thy grace and heavenly aid To fill the hearts which Thou hast made.
 To Thee, the Paraclete, we cry, To Thee, the Gift of God Most High, The Fount of life, the Fire of love, The soul’s Anointing from above.
 The sevenfold gifts of grace are Thine, O Finger of the Hand Divine; The Father’s promise, sent to teach The tongue a rich and heavenly speech.
 Thy light to every sense impart, And shed Thy love in every heart; Thine own unfailing might supply To strengthen our infirmity.
 Drive far away our deadly foe, And grant us Thy true peace to know; So shall we, led by Thee as Guide, Turn from all hurtful steps aside.
 Grant that through Thee the Father we, And eke the Son, may come to know, And Thee, of Both, the Spirit be, Believed while endless ages flow.
 All glory to the Father be, With His co-equal, risen Son, And to the Holy Paraclete, While everlasting ages run.
 Amen
