The priest blesses the rings, singing in Latin:
 V. Our help is in the name of the Lord.
 R. Who made heaven and earth.
 V. O Lord, hear my prayer.
 R. And let my cry come unto thee.
 V. The Lord be with you.
 R. And with your spirit.
 V. Adiutórium nostrum in nómine Dómini.
 R. Qui fecit caelum et terram.

 V. Dómine, exáudi oratiónem meam.
 R. Et clamor meus ad te véniat.

 V. Dóminus vobíscum.
 R. Et cum spíritu tuo.
Prayer  
  Let Us Pray.

Creator and Preserver of the human race, Giver of spiritual grace and Author of eternal salvation, we humbly ask You: graciously bless these rings, which we bless in Your holy name. May those who wear them remain in Your peace, be faithful to Your will, live and grow in Your love, and may they reach old age; and through their numerous offspring may they extend into the distant future. Through Christ our Lord.
 R. Amen.
  Oremus.

Creátor et conservátor géneris humáni, dator grátiae spirituális, cónditor salútis aetérnae: quaesumus, bene dícere dignéris hos ánulos, quos nos in nómine tuo sancto benedícimus; ut qui eos portáverint, in tua pace consístant, in tua voluntáte permáneant, et in tuo amóre vivant, crescant et senéscant atque multiplicéntur in longitúdinem diérum. Per Christum, Dóminum nostrum.
 R. Amen.

Scrutinium

The priest asks the groom:

  N., do you take N., here present for your lawful wife according to the rite of our holy Mother, the Church?

The bridegroom responds:

  I do.

Priest:

  Are you willing to persevere in this union in good times and in bad, until death does you part?

The bridegroom responds:

  I do.

Priest:

  Are you willing to accept and raise in the Catholic faith the children with whom God may bless you?

The bridegroom responds:

  I do.
