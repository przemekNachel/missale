Then he asks the bride:

  N., do you take N., here present for your lawful wife according to the rite of our holy Mother, the Church?

The bride responds:

  I do.

Priest:

  Are you willing to persevere in this union in good times and in bad, until death does you part?

The bride responds:

  I do.

Priest:

  Are you willing to accept and raise in the Catholic faith the children with whom God may bless you?

The bride responds:

  I do.

Priest: 

 May Almighty God grant you His grace, so that what you are about to declare may become the guiding principle of your married life.

Contracting marriage

Priest says: 

 Join your right hands.

Then, with the right end of the stole, he wraps the joined hands of the spouses in the form of a cross and instructs them to pronounce the words by which the marriage is consummated, saying: 

 In order to enter into the holy bond of marriage, repeat after me the words of the vow.

The bridegroom repeats after the priest: 

I, N., TAKE YOU, N., FOR MY LAWFUL WIFE
AND I PLEDGE TO YOU LOVE, FAITHFULNESS
AND MARITAL HONESTY,
AND THAT I WILL NOT ABANDON YOU UNTIL DEATH.
SO HELP ME,
ALMIGHTY GOD,
ONE IN THE HOLY TRINITY, AND ALL THE SAINTS.

The bridegroom repeats after the priest: 

I, N., TAKE YOU, N., FOR MY LAWFUL HUSBAND
AND I PLEDGE TO YOU LOVE, FAITHFULNESS
AND MARITAL HONESTY,
AND THAT I WILL NOT ABANDON YOU UNTIL DEATH.
SO HELP ME,
ALMIGHTY GOD,
ONE IN THE HOLY TRINITY, AND ALL THE SAINTS.
