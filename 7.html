9
After the psalm is finished, the priest sings: 

 V. Lord, have mercy.
 R. Christ, have mercy. Lord, have mercy.

The priest says: 

 Let us pray as our Lord Jesus Christ has taught us.

All recite together: 

Our Father.

Then the priest sings: 
 V. Salvos fac servos tuos.
 R. Deus meus, sperántes in te.

 V. Mitte eis, Dómine, auxílium de sancto.
 R. Et de Sion tuére eos.
 V. Esto eis, Dómine, turris fortitúdinis.
 R. A fácie inimíci.
 V. Dómine, exáudi orationem meam.
 R. Et clamor meus ad te véniat.

 V. Dóminus vobiscum.
 R. Et cum spiritu tuo.
 V. Save Thy servants.
 R. Who put their trust in Thee, my God.
 V. Send them help, O Lord, from Thy holy place.
 R. And defend them out of Sion.
 B. Be unto them, O Lord, a tower of strength.

 R. From the face of the enemy.
 V. O Lord, hear my prayer.
 R. And let my cry come unto Thee.
 V. The Lord be with you.
 R. And with thy spirit.
Prayer  
  Oremus.

Réspice, quaesumus, Dómine, super hos fámulos tuos: et institútis tuis, quibus propagatiónem humáni géneris ordinásti, benígnus assíste; ut, qui te auctóre iungúntur, te auxiliánte servéntur. Per Christum, Dóminum nostrum.
  Let us pray.

Look down, we beseech Thee, O Lord, upon these Thy servants, and graciously protect the institution which Thou hast ordained for the propagation of the human race. May those who are joined together by Thy authority be preserved by Thy help. Through Christ our Lord.
