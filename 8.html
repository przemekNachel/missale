Ordo Missæ

Nuptial Mass

 The Holy Mass is the making present of the sacrifice of Jesus Christ on the cross in an unbloody manner upon the altar. It is propitiatory in nature. Christ offers Himself to make satisfaction for the sins of men.

Mass of the Catechumens

 The priest, having stood at the foot of the altar, signs himself with the sign of the cross, recites the antiphon, and bowing low with the server, recites the general confession.
 Then the priest ascends the steps of the altar.
 The incense, which is consumed, and the smoke rising upwards are symbols of our prayers and sacrifices. The priest incenses the altar, then the server incenses the celebrant as the representative of Christ. During this time the choir sings the antiphon:
