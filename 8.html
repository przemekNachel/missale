Prayer  
  Let Us Pray.

  We ask You, O Lord, look upon these Your servants and graciously support the union You have established for the propagation of the human race. May those who are united by Your will persevere happily with Your help. Through Christ our Lord.

  Oremus.

Réspice, quaesumus, Dómine, super hos fámulos tuos: et institútis tuis, quibus propagatiónem humáni géneris ordinásti, benígnus assíste; ut, qui te auctóre iungúntur, te auxiliánte servéntur. Per Christum, Dóminum nostrum.

Ordo Missæ

Nuptial Mass

 The Holy Mass is the representation of the sacrifice of Jesus Christ on the cross, in an unbloody manner on the altar. It has a propitiatory character. Christ offers Himself to atone for the sins of humanity.

Mass of the Catechumens

 The priest, standing at the steps of the altar, makes the sign of the cross, recites an antiphon, and, deeply bowed, together with the altar server, says the general confession.
 Then the priest ascends the steps of the altar.
 The incense that is burned, and the smoke rising upwards, are symbols of our prayers and offerings. The priest incenses the altar, then the deacon incenses the celebrant as a representative of Christ. Meanwhile, the choir sings the antiphon:

Kyrie - Supplicatory Invocation

 The Kyrie is a short litany originating from the Greek liturgy. It consists of a threefold plea for help directed to each of the Three Persons of the Most Holy Trinity.
