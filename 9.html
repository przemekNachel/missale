Collect - The Prayer of the Church

The priest kisses the altar, then greets the faithful, inviting them to prayer. 

 S. Dóminus vobíscum.
 M. Et cum spíritu tuo.
 S. Orémus.
 K. The Lord be with you.
 W. And with thy spirit.
 K. Let us pray.

The priest returns to the missal to read the solemn prayer of the Church, also called the collect, or the prayer of the assembly. In our name, the priest asks God to look upon the needs of His people, grant them grace, and lead them happily to eternal life.

Exaudi nos, omnipotens et misericors Deus : ut quod nostro ministratur officio, tua benediction potius impleatur. Per Dóminum nostrum Iesum Christum Fílium tuum, qui tecum vivit et regnat in unitáte Spíritus Sancti Deus, per ómnia sǽcula sæculórum.

 M. Amen.
Graciously hear us, almighty and merciful God: that what is performed by our humble ministry may be fulfilled by Thy blessing. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, Thy Son, who liveth and reigneth with Thee in the unity of the Holy Ghost, God, world without end.
 W. Amen.

Epistle

God answers our prayers, instructing us through His inspired word about Himself and our duties toward Him.
 The first reading is taken from the Scriptures of the Old Testament or from the Epistles and Acts of the Apostles.
